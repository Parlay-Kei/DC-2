# Direct Cuts - Keyword Strategy

## Executive Summary

This document outlines the keyword strategy for Direct Cuts' App Store Optimization. The strategy balances high-volume competitive terms with long-tail differentiators to maximize discoverability while competing against established players (Booksy, StyleSeat, Squire, The Cut).

**Primary Differentiators to Emphasize:**
1. Mobile barber service (come to you)
2. Low-fee marketplace (fair pricing)
3. Verified professionals (trust without background check claims)

---

## Competitor Keyword Analysis

### Booksy (Market Leader)
**Strengths:** Dominates "salon booking," "beauty appointment"
**Keywords They Own:** salon, beauty, spa, appointment booking
**Gap Opportunity:** They focus on salons - "barber" terms less optimized
**Strategy:** Position as barber-specific alternative

### StyleSeat
**Strengths:** Strong in "beauty professional," "stylist"
**Keywords They Own:** stylist, beauty pro, salon near me
**Gap Opportunity:** Less focus on male grooming
**Strategy:** Own "mens grooming," "barber" vertical

### Squire
**Strengths:** "Barbershop management," "barber software"
**Keywords They Own:** barbershop pos, barber management
**Gap Opportunity:** B2B focused, consumer discovery weak
**Strategy:** Win consumer-facing "find barber" queries

### The Cut
**Strengths:** "Black barbershop," community focus
**Keywords They Own:** Black barber, urban barbershop
**Gap Opportunity:** Niche focused, general terms available
**Strategy:** Broader market appeal while respecting their niche

### Keyword Gap Summary:

| barber comes to you | All competitors | Unique selling point |
| low fee barber | All competitors | Pricing model |
| verified barber | Most competitors | Trust positioning |
